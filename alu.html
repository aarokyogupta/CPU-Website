Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU)

The Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) is the part in the CPU that deals with arithmetic and logical operations on binary numbers. The ALU is responsible for performing operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, AND, OR, NOT, and XOR. The ALU takes two binary numbers, the operation being performed and a status (flags like overflow, negative or zero) as input and produces a single binary number and a new status output based on the operation being performed. The ALU is a fundamental component of the CPU and is used in almost all computational tasks.
