Clock

The clock is a fundamental part of the computer system that is used to synchronize parts of the CPU together. The clock generates a series of electrical signals that are used to control the timing of the operations in the computer system. The clock signal is used to synchronize the operations of the CPU, memory, buses, and other components to ensure that they work together in a coordinated manner. The clock signal is generated by a crystal oscillator and is used to control the speed of the computer system. The clock speed is measured in Hertz (Hz) and determines how fast the computer system can perform operations. The clock speed is an important factor in determining the performance of the computer system and is used to measure the speed of the CPU and other components.
